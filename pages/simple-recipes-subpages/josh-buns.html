Joshua Weissman's recipe for delicious burger buns

Before we actually make our delectable burger buns, we must first have our ingredients ready!

Ingredients

Overall you will need:

  • A sauce pan
  • A whisk
  • A stand mixer, perferably one with a dough hook attachment
  • A towel
  • A work surface
  • A bowl and something to grease it with
  • 2 Rimed baking sheet tray that are the same size
  • A cooking brush
  • An oven
  • A stove

For the Tangzhong, you will need:

  • 2 tablespoons, or 20 grams, of bread flour
  • 2 tablespoons, or 27 grams, of water
  • 4 tablespoons, or 60 grams, of whole milk

For the dough, you will need:

  • 1/2 cup, or 120 grams, of whole milk, doesn't have to be whole milk, can be non-dairy milk as well
  • 1 tablespoons, or 9 grams, of instant yeast
  • 5/2 cups, or 320 grams, of Bread Flour
  • 1 teapsoon, or 7 grams, of fine sea salt
  • 5/2 tablespoons, or 35 grams, of granulated sugar
  • 1 whole egg
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 3 tablespoons, or 42 grams, of unsalted butter, softened

For the Egg Wash, you will need:

  • 1 whole egg
  • A splash of whole milk

Directions

For the Tangzhong, you will need to:

  1. Add all the Tanhzhong ingredients together in your sauce pan
  2. Place your sauce pan on the stove and whisk your ingredients together
  3. Turn on your stove to about medium heat
  4. Continually whisk your ingredients together until your mixture thickens dramatically, or becomes a paste

For the Dough:

  1. Grab your 1/2 cup, or 120 grams, of milk and heat it to about 95 degrees fahrenheit
  2. Add your 1 tablespoon, or 9 grams, of instant yeast to your milk and stir it
  3. Leave your Milk-yeast mixture in a warm area to sit for about 8 minutes
  4. Grab your stand mixer bowl and add your 5/2 cups, or 320 grams, of bread flour, 1 teapsoon, or 7 grams, of fine sea salt, and 5/2 tablesppons, or 35 grams, of granulated sugar and whisk those ingredients together to ensure they are throughly incoporated
  5. Place the stand mixer bowl back into the stand mixer, and make sure your stand mixer has its dough hook attachment on and turn your stand mixer on at low speed
  6. While your standm mixer is running, go ahead and slowly pour in your milk-yeast mixture and let it run for a few seconds
  7. Add your Tangzhong paste in
  8. Add in one whole egg, preferably at room temperature
  9. Add in one egg yolk, preferably at room temperature
  10. Increase your stand mixer speed from low to medium-low
  11. Then mix everything until it's throughly incoporated
  12. You will most likely need to scrape down the sides when it's mixing, so when you do so ensure the stand mixer is off to ensure your safety, and that you do not lose any fingers I am not liable for any body parts you lose during this process
  13. Once incorporated gradully add 3 tablespoons, or 42 grams, of unsalted, soft butter, don't add it all in at once as it will take longer to incoporate if you do so
  14. Once everything is incorporate continue to let the stand mixer run for about 5 minutes, or until the dough is nice and smooth
  15. Now take your bowl and lightly grease it
  16. Now take your dough and gently fold and place it seam side down on your work surface and sort of rub it around in a circle keeping constant constant contact with the board so that it folds in on itself and creates nice tension in the dough
  17. Place your dough into yourgreased bowl
  18. Grab your towel and wet it
  19. Place your damp towel on top of your bowl to cover your dough
  20. Place your towel covered bowl in a warm area for about 1 to 1 and a half hours, you can place the the towel-covered bowl in a turned off oven with the lights on for a warm enviorment
  21. Once the dough has doubled punch your dough down to deflate it
  22. Gently flour your work surface
  23. Place your dough on the floured work surface
  24. Divide your dough into 6 even pieces, to ensure they are accurately even each dough should weigh about 95-105 grams each
  25. Grab your a section of your dough and gently stretch and fold it towards the center repeat that around the whole segment of dough then flip it over so it's seam side down
  26. Now you can give the dough tension by pulling the dough towards you by keeping constatnt contact with your work surface
  27. Rotate your dough 90 degrees and repeat the previous step
  28. Continue doing the previous 2 steps until your dough is nice and circular
  29. Repeat the last 3 steps for all 6 of your burger bun doughs
  30. Place all 6 of your dough balls evenly spaced out on a rimmed baking sheet tray lined with lightly greased parchment paper
  31. Ensure that the dough balls are spaced apart enough, and they have about 2 and a half inches or six centimeters apart
  32. Once spaced apart enough, place another equally sized rimmed baking sheet, inverted,on top of your current baking sheet to keep them from drying out and allowing them to rise
  33. Allow them to rise at about room temperature for about 1 to 2 hours or until they've at least doubled in size
